Ever wonder why your flight price changes every time you check it?

It's not random, and it's not a trick. It's 35-year-old airline math called
revenue management — and I built a free, open-source tool that lets you play
with it yourself.

The whole problem in one sentence: an empty seat is worth $0 the second the
plane leaves the gate, but not every customer values that seat the same way —
so the price has to keep adjusting as seats sell and the clock runs down.

Same pattern shows up in hotel rooms, concert tickets, cloud compute, surge
pricing, and ad inventory — anywhere you have perishable capacity and uneven
demand.

I wrote up how it works (no math background needed) and built an interactive
demo where you can drag sliders and watch the price curve, load factor, and
revenue react instantly:
